\qns{Review: Four Fundamental Subspaces}

Any $m\times n$ matrix $\mathbf{A}$ has four subspaces:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Column Space:} $C(\mathbf{A})$ consists of all combinations of the columns of $\mathbf{A}$ and is a vector space in $\R^{m}$
	\item \textbf{Nullspace:} $N(\mathbf{A})$ consists of all solutions $\vec{x}$ to the equation $\mathbf{A}\vec{x} = 0$, and lies in $\R^{n}$.
	\item \textbf{Row Space:} $C(\mathbf{A^T})$ The combinations of the row vectors of $\mathbf{A}$, lies in $\R^{n}$. The rowspace is equal to, and represented as, the column space of the transpose of $\mathbf{A}$.
	\item \textbf{Left Nullspace:} $N(\mathbf{A^T})$ consists of all solutions $\vec{x}$ such that $\mathbf{A^T}\vec{x} = 0$, and lies in $\R^{m}$.
\end{itemize}
